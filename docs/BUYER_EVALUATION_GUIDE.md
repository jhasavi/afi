# AdvisorFlow buyer evaluation guide

This guide is for an advisor, team lead, brokerage owner, or potential buyer evaluating whether AdvisorFlow is worth using or acquiring.

## One-sentence product test

AdvisorFlow is useful if it causes advisors to complete more high-quality personal follow-ups every week without replacing their CRM.

## Who it is for

- Real estate brokers and agents with past clients, leads, and referral partners
- Mortgage advisors who need compliant partner and borrower follow-up
- Small teams that already have a CRM but lack daily outreach discipline
- Brokerage operators who want simple weekly visibility without enterprise CRM complexity

## Who it is not for

- Teams looking for a full transaction management system
- Companies that want automated bulk email campaigns
- Businesses that need MLS search or consumer property search
- Advisors who do not have a relationship database yet

## Core value proposition

Most CRMs store names. AdvisorFlow turns the contact book into daily action:

1. Who should I contact today?
2. Why does this person matter now?
3. What should I say?
4. What is the next follow-up date?
5. Did the touch actually happen?

## What to demo

1. **Business review**: show health score, overdue count, active opportunities, stale warm relationships, and weekly actions.
2. **Today**: show ranked outreach cards with reason, next step, and draft.
3. **Message draft**: generate an email/text and show compliance-safe wording.
4. **Log as sent/contacted**: show how the contact record updates.
5. **Import**: show CSV import, NB sync, and HubSpot export/sync.
6. **Weekly review**: show overdue and stale relationship cleanup.

## Buyer questions and answers

| Question | Strong answer |
| --- | --- |
| Is this a CRM replacement? | No. It is a daily action layer that works above a CRM. |
| Does it send messages automatically? | No. Draft-first by default; explicit send/log actions only. |
| What makes it different from ChatGPT? | It ranks contacts, uses relationship context, tracks outcomes, and fits a repeatable workflow. |
| What makes it different from HubSpot? | HubSpot is better for newsletters and campaigns; AdvisorFlow is for personal 1:1 follow-up. |
| Why would advisors keep using it? | It gives a short list every morning and removes blank-page friction. |

## Adoption metrics

| Metric | Target |
| --- | --- |
| Time to first contact list | Under 5 minutes |
| Advisors working Today 3+ days/week | 60%+ of weekly active users |
| Logged touches per advisor/week | 10+ |
| Draft-to-log rate | 40%+ |
| Overdue follow-up trend | Down over 4 weeks |

## Current product strengths

- Daily ranked outreach workflow
- Business Review page for operator-level visibility
- Template mode without OpenAI dependency
- Optional OpenAI mode with safety guardrails
- NB Mission Control integration path
- HubSpot newsletter export/API sync
- Team-plan email send through NB mail when configured
- Tests covering scoring, guardrails, billing, HubSpot, NB email, and business review logic

## Current product risks

- It needs real advisor usage data to prove retention.
- Integration value depends on clean CRM data.
- Newsletter and unsubscribe compliance should remain in HubSpot for now.
- NB write-back/send endpoints must exist in Mission Control production for the full Team workflow.
- Mobile ergonomics should be validated with real advisors before broad rollout.

## Best next validation plan

Run a 4-week pilot with 2-3 advisors:

1. Import or sync 200+ real contacts.
2. Require Today usage 3 days per week.
3. Review Business Review every Friday.
4. Track touches, replies, meetings, referrals, and overdue trend.
5. Interview users after week 2 and week 4.

If advisors keep using it after the novelty fades, the product is worth expanding.
